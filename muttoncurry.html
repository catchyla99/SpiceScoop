Diwali Mutton Curry

Ingredients

  1. For Marination:

    • 500 gms Mutton on the Bone
    • 1 cup Dahi (Curd)
    • 1″ inch Cinnamon Stick
    • 5 – 6 Green Cardamom (Choti Elaichi)
    • 1 Brown Cardamom (Badi Elaichi)
    • 5 Cloves (Laung)
    • 8-10 Whole Peppercorns (Kali Mirch Sabut)
    • 2 tblsp Ginger Garlic Green Chillies paste
    • 1 tblsp Coriander Powder (Dhania powder )
    • 1/4 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi Powder)
    • 1 tsp Garam Masala
    • 1 tsp Red Chilli Powder (Lal Mirch Powder)
    • 2 cup Ghee / Vegetable Oil
    • Salt to taste
  2. For the Curry:

    • 4 cup Hot Water
    • 2 blades Mace (Javitri)
    • 1/8th Nutmeg (Jaiphal)
    • 1 1/2 cup roughly chopped Onions
    • 1 Big tsp Kewra Water
    • 5 – 6 Ginegr Julienne for garnish

Steps

  • In a kadai, heat 1 tblsp oil and fry the onions on medium flame till dark brown. But do not over fry or they will give bitter taste the curry. Take them out on a kitchen paper and drian the extra oil.
  • Once cool, grind them into a rough paste. Do not make a fine paste. Coasreness is important for the right texture.
  • Slightly beat the curd smooth.
  • In a big bowl, put all the ingredients under the marination section together and mix well so that all the mutton pieces are evenly marinated with the mixure. Marinate for at least an hour and for better result overnight.
  • If you are making this mutton curry for a party then marinating a day earlier will not only reduce your work on the party day but will add excellent flavor to your curry.
  • At this stage do not add the brown onion paste, water, javitri, jaiphal and the kewra water.
  • Later, put a big deep kadhai / pan on heat and tip in the marinated mutton curry.
  • Let the mutton fry for 15 – 20 mins on medium flame. Keep stirring from time to time.
  • After some 20 – 25 mins you will find that the mutton has turned light brown and ghee / oil begins to separate from the sides.
  • At this stage add the brown onion paste and cook for another 5 minutes stirring constantly.
  • Now add the hot water and let the mutton cook for anothetr 20 – 25 minutes. Cover the pan with the lid. Do remember to stir every 5 minutes.
  • Meanwhile dry roast the mace blades and nutmeg in a pan and then make a fine powder of it with motar pestle. You can add the nutmeg and mace just like that. But if you prefer a smooth curry with no whole spices coming in your mouth, then it is advisable to ground them to a powder. Also remember not to add too much of these spices as they have very very stong affect and can add bitter taste to the curry. So stick to the quantity adviced in the recipe.
  • After 20 minutes add the nutmeg- mace powder and mix well. Cook for another 10 mins with close lid. Stir once or twice.
  • Later open the lid and check if the mutton has tenderised. If not then cook some more. And if the water has completely evaporated them add 1 cup of Hot water and cook some more for the mutton to get tender.
  • If you want to reduce the cooking time then move the mutton to a pressure cooker and give 4 – 5 whistles.
  • Once the mutton is cooked properly add the kewra water and julienned ginger.
  • Let the mutton rest for atleast and hour to get maximum flavor.
  • Serve with rumali roti and slice onions.
